Literature

The Sangam literature can be considered as the ancient predecessor of Malayalam.[608] Malayalam literature starts from the Old Malayalam period (9thâ€“13th century CE) and includes such notable writers as the 14th-century Niranam poets and the 16th-century poet Thunchaththu Ezhuthachan, whose works mark the dawn of both the modern Malayalam language and its poetry. Malayalam literature has been presented with 6 Jnanapith awards, the second-most for any Dravidian language and the third-highest for any Indian language.

THEATRE

Theatre

Malayalam films carved a niche for themselves in the Indian film industry with the presentation of social themes. Since the 1980s, actors Mohanlal and Mammootty have dominated the movie industry. Mohanlal has won five National Film Awards (four for acting), while Mammootty has three National Film Awards for acting. As of 2018, Malayalam cinema has got 14 awards for the best actor, 6 for the best actress, 11 for the best film, and 13 for the best film director in the National Film Awards, India.

MUSIC

Music

The development of classical music in Kerala is attributed to the contributions it received from the traditional performance arts associated with the temple culture of Kerala. The development of the indigenous classical music form, Sopana Sangeetham, illustrates the rich contribution that temple culture has made to the arts of Kerala. Carnatic music dominates Keralite traditional music. Raga-based renditions known as sopanam accompany kathakali performances. Panchavadyam is a form of percussion ensemble, in which artists use five types of percussion instruments. Kerala's visual arts range from traditional murals to the works of Raja Ravi Varma, the state's most renowned painter.
