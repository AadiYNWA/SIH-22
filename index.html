Literature

The Bengali language boasts a rich literary heritage and has a long tradition in folk literature. Some of the famous works are Charyapada, Mangalkavya, Shreekrishna Kirtana, Thakurmar Jhuli and was modernised by works of various authors such as Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay, Michael Madhusudan Dutt, Rabindranath Tagore, Kazi Nazrul Islam, and Sharat Chandra Chattopadhyay.

THEATRE

Theatre

Jatra is the traditional folk dance of West Bengal. Its long tradition of art films includes globally acclaimed film directors such as Academy Award-winning director Satyajit Ray, Ritwik Ghatak, Mrinal Sen, Tapan Sinha, Aparna Sen, Buddhadeb Dasgupta, Goutam Ghose, Koushik Ganguly, Rituparno Ghosh, Anjan Dutt, Kamaleswar Mukherjee and Sandip Ray.

MUSIC

Music

Baul tradition is a unique heritage of Bengali folk music, which has been influenced by regional traditions. Folk music in West Bengal is often accompanied by the ektara, a one-stringed instrument. The state is recognised for its appreciation of rabindrasangeet and Indian classical music. Nazrul Geeti is another classical music of Bengal, which is written and composed by poet Kazi Nazrul Islam. UNESCO selected The Rural Craft Hub of Bengal to showcase their artwork in Paris in 2015.
